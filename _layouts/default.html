Nico Schiavone

I am an M.Sc. student at the University of Toronto, Department of Computer Science, advised by Sheila McIlraith. I am interested in multi-agent systems; I research cooperative AI with classical and LLM based agents. I also work in LLM steering and planning (especially long-term planning and goal decomposition).

I graduated with a B.Sc. in Engineering Physics with a Minor in Mathematics from the University of Alberta in Spring 2024. I focused on medical computer vision, with work on efficient algorithms for deep learning and image synthesis frameworks. I also briefly worked on superconductivity and mathematical physics problems.

I was fortunate to be advised by Xingyu Li, Frank Marsiglio, and Tian Tang over the course of my degree.

During my B.Sc., I worked as a software engineer at TELUS and a researcher at TRIUMF. Outside of academia, I am an avid chess fan, writer, weightlifter and bedroom DJ.
