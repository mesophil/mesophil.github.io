Nico Schiavone

I am an M.Sc. student in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Toronto, advised by Sheila McIlraith and Eldan Cohen. I am interested in multi-agent systems and cooperation, especially with respect to mechanism design, algorithms, and LLM agents. Currently, I research problems in cooperative AI at the intersection of computer science, statistics, and economics.

In 2024, I graduated Mathematics and Engineering Physics at the University of Alberta. I was fortunate to be advised by Xingyu Li, Frank Marsiglio, and Tian Tang.

In the past, I was an internal tools software engineer at TELUS and a physics researcher at TRIUMF. Most days, I enjoy drawing, writing, lifting, and mixing house. I'm also currently working on a comic.

In Summer 2025, I will be interning at Microsoft, working on Copilot Agents.

If you are interested in multi-agent systems and cooperation, or just want to chat, feel free to reach out by any avenue.
